In honor of Joan Feynman: Women in astrophysics

Mon, January 12

Triple Peaks Library Conference Hall & Online

At this event, which celebrates the library's namesake, our guest lecturer will take us on a journey exploring some of the most prominent female researchers in the world of space science. It's easy to buy into the misconception that this field was mostly dominated by men, but our lecture aims to change that notion and prove that this hasn't been the case since at least the 18th century.

That our readers love

  • Snow Crash

    Inspired by a technological error mode like TV static, this intriguing sci-fi cyberpunk novel tells a story about a dystopian future theming around many themes - ranging from history and philosophy to computer science and cryptography - and everything in between.

    Neal Stephenson

  • Philosophy: Who Needs It

    A collection of academic essays, exploring the foundations, necessities, and occurrences of various philosophies in our everyday life - how they're assumed to be the central role deep-rooted into humanity - and the founding points of Objectivism. Included are periodicals, open letters and bibliographical anecdotes from the graduate of the US Military Academy - the author herself.

    Ayn Rand

  • A New Approach to Logic in the Humanities

    Logic proofing doesn't have to be tied down to mathematics or coding! Turns out, logic happens all around us without even noticing; applied one too many times within the various fields of study, down to debatable philosophical constructs like consciousness and multiple realities.

    Prof. Chad Hansen

  • Lost & Adrift

    The blurb for this rather classic romantic novel is that it tells the story of a captain sailing for a new adventure out at sea and meeting up with an ideal first mate, also adrift - into a long burning, drawn out, and surprisingly wholesome best-seller out there.
    Note: This is a meta story thought of by the DM of the DnD campaign "Heart of Elynthi".

    Chingles McBallister, JoCat

About the library

Libraries are essential establishments in practically every city, and the Triple Peaks Library is no exception. It first opened its doors way back in 2018, right as our humble town began bidding welcome to its very first citizens!

Since its initial opening, the library has seen nearly 342 monthly visitors. That's almost 50% of our population! So far, most citizens have enjoyed reading books and using our ample online resources.

Become a member today, and make sure to check out our Staff Picks section. This week's selection is full of books anyone interested in computer programming would love!
